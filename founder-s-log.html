The Prime Directive

The visionary writer Gene Roddenberry showed us a future where Humankind had handled our “issues” to so great an extent, that we had enough extra time on our hands to build a Star Fleet.

By that time, Humans had learned a lot. So much so that Star Fleet’s NUMBER ONE RULE was aimed directly at what Gene Roddenberry saw as Human History’s main error - INTERFERENCE.

In Trekkie parlance, the name of this top rule is

The Prime Directive and it basically says - don’t ever interfere with the development of others.

Stack that on top of the premise of Star Fleet (exploration), and what you’ve got is this…

As we explore the heavens, we can observe foreigners, not exploit them. Don’t even startle them. Certainly above all… do not be a hinderance to them. Don’t roll up with your fancy ship and wickedly devised weapons, and lean on a people.

Trekies know this well and I’ll always be grateful to my Uncle Reg for not only introducing me to Star Trek as an 80’s kid, but explaining the Prime Directive to me from his perspective as A Navy Man.

And I’m sure many Trekkies like me have thought about the implications of the PRIME directive as it applies to non-fiction Humanity as well. In a way, Trekkies may understand more than anyone that the imbalances caused by the Prime Directive violations of European “Explorers” have had unacceptable fallouts worldwide, that need to be addressed as a PRIME priority if we are to call ourselves a developed or intelligent species.
