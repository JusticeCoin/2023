Problem: 

A Colonial World-Culture in Which: 


  • • Blacks of the American Experience continue to endure psychological abuse related to the most funded productions in history.
  • • BLAMEX Creators have been limited by the  historical sabotage of works through the subversion of Black Expression, and poverty.
  • • Compounding the psychological assault of forced economic imbalances, Blacks have heretofore been limited to cultural validation through historically racist institutions. 


Solution: 

A Ministry of Sound & Image Circulation, that: 


  • • Provides guidelines for all people, pertaining to portraying Blacks of the American Experience in their motion pictures.
  • • Provides resources that empower otherwise marginalized Topian Survivors to realize world class productions.
  • • In accordance with internal standards and interests, acknowledge and celebrate the achievements of artists past and present, who exemplify Topian potential.
